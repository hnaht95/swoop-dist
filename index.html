What it does

Everything a browser download can't.

Point Swoop at a page and it figures out the rest - the real video, the right audio, a format your Mac can open.

Grab any video or file

Direct files, and streaming video split into hundreds of fragments - Swoop captures it and stitches it back into one clean file.

One click from your browser

A Download button appears right on the video. Or right‑click anything and choose Send to Swoop. Chrome, Edge, Brave.

Plays on your Mac, guaranteed

Downloaded a VP9 or AV1 clip QuickTime chokes on? Swoop quietly converts only what your Mac can't play to H.264.

Lives in your menu bar

A tiny widget shows progress at a glance - that signature curve. Pause, resume, reveal in Finder, then it slips away.

Built for streaming sites

Merges separate audio and video, retries fragments that drop, and picks the quality you set - up to the max the site offers.

Fast, native, quiet

A real Mac app - not a browser in disguise. Universal binary, light on memory, no telemetry, no sign‑in.

Three steps

From a page you're watching to a file you own.

STEP 01

Install Swoop

Drag it to Applications and add the browser helper. About a minute - no account, no setup.

STEP 02

Hit download

Click Download on any video, or right‑click and Send to Swoop. The menu‑bar widget picks it up.

STEP 03

It swoops in

Swoop merges, converts if needed, and drops a ready‑to‑play file in your Downloads.
